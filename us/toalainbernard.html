History of the Hamburger

The use of minced meat in the shape of hamburger can be found throughout history, with one of the earliest references in the journals of Genghis Khan's armies.

Because his men were always on the road, riding their horses, they had to have an easy way to eat. They placed their meat under their saddles to tender it, and they would eat it raw. These men called their burgers tartars.
